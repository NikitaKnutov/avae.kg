Spot currency trading attracts traders due to its accessibility and ability to quickly respond to changes in market conditions. However, it requires a deep understanding of the market and risk management, as volatility can lead to both significant gains and losses.

A foreign exchange transaction (or conversion) is a transaction involving the exchange of one currency for another. It is carried out in the foreign exchange market and can include various forms of exchange, such as the purchase of foreign currency to pay for goods and services, hedging currency risks or speculative transactions in order to profit from differences in exchange rates.

Liquidity Transfer is the process of moving funds or other liquid assets between different accounts, divisions or branches within the same financial institution or between different financial institutions. The main purpose of such a transfer is to ensure a sufficient level of liquidity to fulfill obligations to clients, counterparties or regulators.

Access to the International EFX Exchange Platform provides financial market participants with the opportunity to transact in foreign currencies in real time. Such platforms bring together buyers and sellers from all over the world, offering a wide selection of currency pairs and convenient trading tools.
